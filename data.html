Who was Mark Dean?

Mark Dean is an african american iventor. He is best known for his work on the first color PC monitor, the ISA system bus, and the first gigahertz chip. Mark Dean did this work at IBM, holding three of the company's original nine patents. His work with Dennis Moeller paved the way for many early plug-in devices like disk drives and printers.

Mark Dean's Life

Dean was born in Jefferson City, Tennesse, on March 2, 1957. As a young boy he would commonly build things and showed interest in engineering, For example, Dean helped to make a tractor from scratch with his father, who worked at the Tennessee Valley Authority. He stood out in school as a gifted athlete and top student with straight As. He would later go on to study at University of Tennesse, where he studied engineering. After this, he took a job at IBM, where he'd work for the most part of his career. He proved to be an important piece of the company, developing some of their earliest and most important technologies.
